1.5 Research Objectives
The primary objective of this research is to examine the status of Respectful Maternity Care in Osun State, Southwest Nigeria, its role as a predictor of women's choice of maternal care and pregnancy outcomes as well as the potential strategies to improve the readiness for change to RMC in Osun State
 Specific objectives include:
1.To determine the current level of knowledge of healthcare providers regarding Respectful Maternity Care in Osun State, Nigeria
2.To determine the current status of RMC practices among healthcare providers in Osun State, Southwest Nigeria.
3.To explore the perceptions of women about RMC during childbirth in Osun State.
4.To determine the proportion of women experiencing respectful maternity care (RMC) during childbirth in Osun State
5.To identify factors influencing the practice of RMC among healthcare providers in Osun State.
6.To determine the factors influencing women's choice of maternal care in the region.
7.To evaluate the organizational and individual readiness for change to RMC in the study location. 
8.To identify potential strategies to improve the readiness for a change to RMC practice in Osun State.



1.6 Research Hypotheses
1. Null Hypotheses (H0):
H0: The status of Respectful Maternity Care has no significant influence on the choice of maternal care among women in Southwest Nigeria.
H0: The status of Respectful Maternity Care has no significant effect on pregnancy outcomes among women in Southwest Nigeria.
Ho: The level of knowledge of health care providers on RMC has no significant influence on the status of respectful maternity care in Osun State Nigeria
Ho:  Individual healthcare provider’s readiness for change to RMC practices has no significant influence on the status of RMC in Osun State, Nigeria.
Ho:  Organizational readiness for change to RMC practices has no significant influence on the status of RMC in Osun State, Nigeria
Ho; The socio-demographic status of women has no significant influence on the maternal perception of D&A during childbirth among women in Osun State
